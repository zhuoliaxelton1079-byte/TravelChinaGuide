Visa & Document Guidance

Use this as your document and travel-admin checkpoint before departure. Final approval still comes from your program office and current consular guidance.

  • Carry passport, visa paperwork, and flight reservation confirmations in both print and digital form.
  • Bring a backup government ID (for example, driver's license) separate from your passport wallet.
  • Contact your credit card company before departure to confirm international transaction policies and fees.
  • Contact your phone provider to confirm roaming/international data options and pricing.
  • Set up core travel apps before you fly: WeChat (a messaging and payment super-app essential for daily communication in China), Alipay (a mobile payment app widely used across China for cashless transactions), DiDi, and an offline translation tool such as Apple Translate (built into iPhone — download the Chinese language pack for offline use).
  • Keep a group emergency contact sheet in your day bag and saved in your phone.
